Defining a Genome-wide TR Catalog

Most existing methods for genotpying tandem repeats (TRs) in short-read or long-read sequencing data require the user to specify the exact start and end coordinates and motifs of all TR loci to genotype. Therefore, a key step in any genome-wide analysis involves selecting the set of TRs (aka. catalog) to include. This choice directly affects sensitivity since any loci not in the catalog will be missing from downstream analysis, but also compute costs since runtimes of most current tools are proportional to the size of the catalog being genotyped. There is no consensus in the literature on how to approach this choice, and recently published papers have contiued to use a wide range of catalogs.

Catalogs can differ from each other on several important properties, including:

  • total # of loci
  • includes homopolymers?
  • distribution of motif sizes
  • includes only pure, or also impure repeats? and if it includes impure, how often do locus definitions overlap?
  • do loci overlap each other?
